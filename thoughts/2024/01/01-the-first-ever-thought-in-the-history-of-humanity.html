The First Ever Thought In The History of Humanity

Date: 02-07-2024

I'm Cheyenne, and you can learn more about me on my About Me page. To make a long story short, I'm just someone who likes to create things, and is slowly but surely trying to find their place in the world, as well as survive it at the same time. I decided to make this website in late 2023 for a multitude of reasons. I'll talk about them a bit in this post.

To begin, I wanted 2024 to be a year of improvement. Coming off of 2023, it contained probably the biggest steps forward in my life in recent years. After suspecting myself of having ADHD for over a year, I finally was able to be diagnosed and start medication for it. While this didn't totally fix my ADHD, which is very severe, it did help immensely in a lot of ways. I struggle with many mental health issues, and have ever since I was a kid. I didn't have a great childhood at all, which I might delve more into some day, but it's a bit beyond the scope of this initial post.

Anyway, after a few years of pushing parts of myself behind me, I told myself that 2024 was the year that I was done hiding my true self, both from myself and others, throughout most every facet of my life. In recent years I've always been an advocate of such a thing, but I never fully followed my own advice - probably out of insecurity, despite my best attempts. I told myself that in 2024, all of that is over for real. No more hiding from who I am, and what I like. No more hiding that from other people, either. I'm simply me, and I want to enjoy my time on this earth. I don't want to be a shut-in anymore. I don't want to stay mostly miserable.

2024 is the year of the start of my rebirth as a person, or at least that's how I like to imagine it. A few days into 2024, I got really bad Covid, and as of the time of writing this, on February 7th, 2024, it's still fucking me up. Thankfully, most of my symptoms have went away by now - but I'm still dealing with shortness of breath, coughing, and messed up taste buds. Which all really suck, especially with the situation I'm in right now.

Anyway, that's a bit off-topic. The point is, I thought a website would be a cool way to help facilitate that rebirth. To connect with not only myself, but others as well, by writing and reflecting on things - whether they be my thoughts on something important, someone else's creation, my own creations, or whatever else I want to put on this website down the line. Essentially, having my own little space just sounds good. My own little corner of the internet. And, being honest, I live on the Internet... so I want a place of my own on here, too.

I hate how centralized and uniform everything has become online. I miss the days of forums and communities where people knew each-other. I miss the days of people having their own websites, and putting so much of themselves out there. I miss the days before people internalized a sense of shame for being "too out-there". People nowadays always say "be yourself" - but all too often, that just means "be yourself, until it's too weird for us". No. Fuck that. Cringe culture is said to be dead, but it's remnants are deeply rooted into us, and the online world is no exception.

There's a larger cultural trend of a general lack of sincerity these days. Whether it be from our popular media, our celebrities, or whatever else. And I can't stand it. And I refuse it. I want to be one of the people who push against that. And making a website is just one thing I can do to help that. I'm inspired by others who've made their own websites, who still use forums, and have formed communities, even in the era of the over-monetized, sanitized Internet.

The Internet used to be a place of perpetual self-expression. And it was beautiful. That's why it grew so big, and that's why so many people were more happy with it back then. But along the way, it's slowly become corrupted, and corporatized, like most of our society has these days. Which is, needless to say, absolutely terrible, and ruining people's lives and sense of self.

If you want to read more, and learn more about what made me seriously consider creating my own website, I recommend that you check out these posts here: What is the Web Revival?

I've probably forgotten some stuff I wanted to write about here, but there will be plenty of time for that later. I can't promise a consistent schedule for these thoughts, but I'll try to write them whenever I feel inspired to talk about something, whatever that may be.

P.S. - I realize the way I come off here can be misconstrued as if I'm some sort of personal freedoms hero, or something. That's not how I want to come off, don't get me wrong. I understand that me making a website isn't some sort of grand statement. And in all actuality, very, very few people will even visit this website, let alone read this. But, like I said - this isn't only about other people. That's a sort of modern Internet, social media mindset. That creations are dominantly made for others. If you want to live that way, go ahead. I myself try to have a healthy balance of making art for myself first and foremost, and then the joy of sharing that creation with others afterwards. But there absolutely is something to be said about the general loss of perception of creations coming from individuals, and the concept that creations that are first and foremost made for the creators themselves, which is how it has been for all of human history. But now, many things are very monetized - creations are all too often spawned not from passion and love, but the need to create it for profit. Maybe sometimes they still turn out good. But I believe this trend in general is extremely harmful to the human experience.

And remember - the terms artist, creator, musician, director - whatever else; these aren't special, coveted titles that are reserved for special people with perceived talents and innate skills. These are things that everyone can be. Everyone can create anything, and other people will absolutely resonate with it. It doesn't matter what you think your skill level is. Don't be the person that I've been for all too long now. Don't be the person who makes things and never shares the vast majority of them with the world because they "aren't good enough". It's a hard hurdle to clear, but you have to at least try. To push yourself out there.

I don't claim to be perfect in this regard at all. I feel like I struggle with this to an insane amount. Even now, when I'm actively trying to fight against it, it's hard. Sometimes it's out of embarrassment that I don't share things. Sometimes it's because I have such strict standards for myself. And, to be clear, I'm not saying to share stuff you aren't comfortable with sharing. It's not so black-and-white like that. But rather, I say that I think we should all really ask ourselves if we should be ashamed of what we create, and/or who you are.

The answer is pretty much always a resounding no. I believe that one way we can change our world and our future for the better, as well as our larger collective attitude towards... well, everything, is to first accept ourselves. Then we can better understand everyone else.

That's how I envision a better tomorrow being created. That's what I'd like to see in the world.
