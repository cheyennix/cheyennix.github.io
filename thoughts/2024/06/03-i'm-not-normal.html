I'm Not Normal

Date: 06-08-2024

This isn't a new concept.
This isn't a new thought.

The only "normal" to me is what I see on display in others, never myself.

Ever since I was a kid, I didn't fit in. Constantly being in trouble for things I didn't even know were problems, not having some supposed "common sense" that everyone around me did. Causing issues for others, being yelled at by my family, even by strangers at times... It's no wonder that as I grew up, I hated myself more and more. Over time stemming from around the age of 12 or so I began to close myself off from everybody. I sabotaged my own relationships with my friends. I cut them out of my life, even my own girlfriend, who I was very close to at the time (by younger standards, you know the drill). After that was done, I had nobody close left to me in real life. I dove headfirst into my digital life as a result. Making a lot of online friends, suddenly abandoning them... Making even more, and then abandoning them as well a year or two later. The only time I haven't done this in my life thus far is with my current friend group, who I've known since 2017 or so. But I would be lying if I said I didn't get the urge to do it again at times.

It's not because I dislike them. Truthfully, I don't even know why I do it. Why I feel this urge to abandon those closest to me... All I have to offer myself instead is vague suspicions from theories I can think up about myself. The only real one I can imagine is that it serves as a sort of self-punishment. A subconscious feeling that no, I don't deserve closeness. I'm weird and different from you, and if you knew everything I really thought, you'd hate me. And you'd never understand it, and you'd never understand me, and I should just leave because you want to be friends than people better than me, and I want to be friends with people better for me as well. People who I know won't judge me if I show them the actual, 100% honest and truthful me.

Actually, I don't know if those people even exist. And I am plenty close with my current friend group to begin with - I love them. But I guess my heart wants more, it wants true closeness. It wants actual, genuine acceptance and human connection from other people. Even when I feel I am close with others, in my soul I can feel the disconnect between us. I often wonder if other people around me can feel it as well. If they ever get feelings like this, like I so frequently do. Or if it really is only me, and I am even more different than I even thought I was to begin with.

This disconnect stays with me throughout my life. It's a huge dividing line between my existence and everyone else's. I feel I am lacking a sort of spiritual kindred that my soul feels will complete it.

So, what's wrong with me?

Is it that I'm on the autism spectrum? Yeah, that'd make a lot of sense. Not just for these feelings but so, so much about my life over the years ever since I was a kid. It makes me feel less alone knowing that there are others like me. Actually, a few of my friend group seem to be on the spectrum as well, and the friend in which I've felt I could be the most open with throughout the years is already diagnosed. In fact, it often feels like I can be more open with them because they aren't as judging as the other people around me, from my friends to everyone else in the world. Like they are playing with a similar instruction set to me. As if we are still different from each-other, but often times different in ways that make more sense to me. Of course, this is all on a sort of sliding scale. I'd take my friends any day over some stranger, and I'm not really even trying to rank them in any sort of way. Not only that, but just feeling like I can be more open with someone doesn't mean I'm super close to them or anything, anyway. At the end of the day, I love my friends, but I do feel a sort of disconnect there even still despite being able to be closer to them than anyone else in my life. This is a feeling I feel ashamed over, but I know I shouldn't. That'd just be hating myself for simply being myself yet again.

I've also looked at BPD. Yeah, so it turns out that the whole abandoning friend groups and learning to hate yourself thing, as well as constantly flip-flopping mentally about your life, how you feel about your friends, and whatever else is hugely indicative of BPD. I sure fit that to a T. BPD, ADHD and autism actually share a few things in common in this regard, so that complicates pinpointing an answer to the question of "what's wrong with me" even more than it already is and has been for all of my life.

This lack of closeness, this life I've put myself into through simply existing, and the depression and self-hatred I've weaponized subconsciously against myself forever now... I do try to combat it. I've gotten better. I've made improvements. I'm trying to make more. I've seen doctors. I've tried to tackle my largest problems in life over time, and the past year or so has definitely been successful in that regard. But to learn for the first time that it's okay to love myself is such a difficult struggle. To try to fully comprehend that not hating myself doesn't mean that I can't dislike things about me and my life... That the person I mentioned before who I want to exist, who loves me unconditionally and accepts me no matter what, as well as connect to on a truly deeper level? They've already been here, but have been unable to love me due to problems of their own.

That person isn't someone else, though. That person is me. If it is actually true that nobody like that exists, then at the very least, I can try to be that person. For me. For myself. To be the change I want to see in the world, even if that world is as small as my own internal sense of existence and how I perceive the world around me. And I want to be that person for others as well. To make the world a bit less darker for others by simply existing and being myself. To give them the courage to do the same, to never hate themselves again. To shape the ways I see the world in positive ways. Because I do deserve that, and other people deserve that too.

But that's all just something I try to do. It's all something I try to be. It doesn't mean it's impossible, but it doesn't mean it's super easy or that it comes natural to me, either. It's just something I need to keep trying at, I suppose. I don't want to give up on myself like how I gave up on myself in the past again. Like how others gave up on me.

I shouldn't have to hate myself just because I'm different from everyone else. Just because I feel like I don't belong with other people... I deserve to be able to be different in my own ways.

Maybe I think about this so strongly because I'm different from everyone else to begin with.

I don't know. At the end of the day, all I know is that I'm not normal. And while I wouldn't hate others for being the same, applying that same concept to myself is where it gets notably more difficult. So, for the sake of myself, for the sake of my life, for the sake of my creations, I'm trying my best to get better at that. I'm trying my best to not only simply be okay with myself, but to love myself as well. And in a way, posting this is a step towards that, just like making this site in the first place was.

Thank you for reading my rambling vent-channel-type post, random person, if you're out there. Hopefully it was sufficiently based and/or cringe enough to your likings.
